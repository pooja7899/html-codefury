Information About Tsunamis

What is a Tsunami?

A tsunami is a series of large ocean waves caused by the sudden displacement of water, usually due to underwater earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides. Tsunamis can cause extensive damage to coastal areas and pose significant risks to life and property.

Tsunami Safety Tips

  • Know the Warning Signs: Be aware of natural warning signs such as a sudden and unusual withdrawal of the sea or strong ground shaking.
  • Have an Evacuation Plan: Identify and practice evacuation routes leading to higher ground away from the coast.
  • Stay Informed: Listen to official warnings and alerts from local authorities and tsunami warning systems.
  • Move to Higher Ground: If you feel a strong earthquake or receive a tsunami alert, immediately move to higher ground and stay there until the all-clear is given.
  • Do Not Return Early: Do not return to coastal areas until authorities have confirmed it is safe to do so.

Post-Tsunami Safety Measures

  • Check for Injuries: Assess yourself and others for injuries. Provide first aid and seek medical help if needed.
  • Inspect Your Property: Look for structural damage, and avoid entering unstable buildings. Report damage to authorities and your insurance company.
  • Avoid Contaminated Water: Do not drink or use water from potentially contaminated sources.
  • Be Careful of Debris: Watch out for hazardous debris and avoid touching or moving anything that may be dangerous.
  • Stay Updated: Follow local news and official updates for recovery information and further safety instructions.
  • Help Others: Assist neighbors and others in need, especially those who may be more vulnerable or injured.
  • Watch for Aftershocks: Be aware of possible aftershocks and stay alert for additional tsunami waves or other hazards.
  • Practice Hygiene: Maintain good hygiene practices and be cautious of any potential disease outbreaks.
  • Document Damage: Take photos of property damage for insurance claims and recovery efforts.
  • Seek Professional Assistance: Contact professionals for major repairs and ensure that all safety standards are met during repairs.
