Information About Floods

What is a Flood?

A flood occurs when water overflows onto normally dry land, often due to heavy rainfall, river overflow, or storm surges. Floods can cause significant damage to property and pose serious risks to safety.

Flood Safety Tips

  • Stay Informed: Monitor weather forecasts and flood alerts from local authorities.
  • Emergency Kit: Prepare a kit with essentials like water, food, medications, and a flashlight.
  • Evacuation Plan: Know your evacuation routes and have a plan in place for your family and pets.
  • Elevate Belongings: Move valuables and electrical appliances to higher floors to avoid water damage.
  • Do Not Drive: Avoid driving through floodwaters as they may be deeper than they appear and can be hazardous.

Post-Flood Safety Measures

  • Inspect Safety: Check for injuries and provide first aid if needed.
  • Avoid Contaminated Water: Do not drink or use floodwater for cooking or washing.
  • Check Property: Inspect your home for damage and report to your insurance company.
  • Stay Updated: Follow local news for recovery information and safety instructions.
  • Clean Up Safely: Wear protective gear while cleaning up debris and be cautious of mold.
  • Document Damage: Take photos and videos of any damage for insurance claims and recovery records.
  • Check for Structural Damage: Assess your home for any signs of structural issues, such as cracks or weakened foundations.
  • Remove Wet Materials: Remove and discard wet or contaminated materials like carpets and insulation to prevent mold growth.
  • Maintain Hygiene: Wash your hands frequently and ensure all surfaces are sanitized to prevent disease.
  • Seek Professional Help: Contact professionals for repairing significant damage and ensure that all repairs meet safety standards.
