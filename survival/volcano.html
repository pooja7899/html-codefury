Information About Volcanoes

What is a Volcano?

A volcano is a rupture in the Earth's crust where molten lava, ash, and gases can escape. Eruptions can range from minor lava flows to catastrophic explosions, impacting surrounding areas and altering landscapes.

Volcano Safety Tips

  • Know the Risks: Understand the volcanic hazards specific to your area, such as lava flows, ashfall, or pyroclastic flows.
  • Emergency Kit: Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like water, food, masks, and first-aid supplies to protect against ash inhalation.
  • Evacuation Plan: Have a clear evacuation plan and know multiple routes to safety, avoiding areas prone to lava flows and ashfall.
  • Stay Informed: Monitor alerts from local authorities and volcano monitoring services for timely updates and instructions.
  • Protect Your Health: Use masks and protective eyewear to shield yourself from ash and dust. Avoid contact with contaminated water and food.

  • Follow Local Guidance: Adhere to advice and instructions from local authorities and emergency services for recovery and safety.
  • Document Damage: Take photos of property damage for insurance purposes and recovery documentation.
  • Check for Gas Leaks: If you smell gas, turn off the gas supply and contact the gas company immediately.
  • Protect Your Health: Continue using masks and eyewear to protect against lingering ash and dust.
  • Stay Updated: Keep up with recovery efforts and updates from local authorities to stay informed about ongoing risks and safety measures.
  • Help Others: Assist neighbors and community members who may need help, especially those who are more vulnerable.
